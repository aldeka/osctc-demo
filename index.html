Your women-in-CS group can invite people into open source.

Schedule a day to teach version control, bug finding, and other skills with an Open Source Comes to Campus workshop! We (OpenHatch) will provide you with the tools, mentorship, and connections to make it happen.

Welcoming.

Open Source Comes to Campus is a curriculum designed for newcomers to open source. We've honed the workshop over three years of practice with 20 student groups, minimizing social, technical, and knowledge dependencies. Now you can run an inviting event like that, too.

Easy.

As the organizer of a local event, you'll get all the teaching resources you need: slides, hands-on exercises for students, and more. We'll help you find professional programers to help during the event, and curate "bitesize bugs" so your attendees have a gentle on-ramp into a real open source project.

Practical.

Students will rave about the practical skills they'll learn: version control, how to communicate in a big software project, and how open source culture works. They'll gain new connections to professionals active in open source development.

What is Open Source Comes to Campus?

A curriculum built to engage students.

Open Source Comes to Campus is a day-long, hands-on workshop. In the morning, students learn about version control; the history of open source; how open source communities communicate on bug trackers, IRC, and mailing lists; and attend a career panel to understand how open source plays a part in professionals' lives. In the afternoon, students work together with existing open source projects to make real changes to projects people use.

Supported by OpenHatch and the Open Source Initiative

Two national non-profits in open source, OpenHatch and OSI, fund and organize these events. That means as a local organizer, you'll be supported through some of the challenges you'll face: logistics, finding food sponsors, customizing the content, and more.

Free of cost

The curriculum to Open Source Comes to Campus is available for free, under an open source-style license. As a non-profit, OpenHatch relies on donations, so if your student group budget can afford paying a fee, we would be happy to accept it, but we will not turn you away if not.
